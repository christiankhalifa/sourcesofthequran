Qur'an Reference Form

Field Legend ▼

Broad Topic: Name main protagonist (e.g. Musa), event or place this passage relates to.

Focus Point: Specific idea, concept or story under analysis (e.g., Refusal to Nurse).

Qur'anic Reference(s): Qur’anic verse(s) related to the focus point.

Qur'anic Unit: The textual segment in which the verse(s) under analysis are logically or semantically embedded.

Antecedent: Any pre-Qur’anic text useful for arguing that the passage under analysis contains borrowed and/or developed material.

Antecedent Unit: Textual segment that can be considered a comprehensive parallel text to the Qur’anic unit under analysis.

Reference; Link to Antecedent: Bibliographic citation and/or URL linking to the antecedent text; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;).

Alternative Titles: List all language traditions of the antecedent under analysis.

Broad Literary Category: General classification of the antecedent.

Occasion for (Intentional or Unintentional) Misinterpretation: Insert Biblical verse(s) with mode of reference (lifted, quoted, cited or alluded to within the antecedent); separate multiple entries with semicolons (;).

Embellishments: Features within the Qur’anic antecedent that led to this developmental interpretation.

Linguistic Distortion: Inserting wrong vowels.

Eisegesis: Quoting out of context.

Presumed Original Language: The original language in which the antecedent text was likely composed; include name of codicologist and/or short citation in brackets; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;)

Presumed Place of Origin: The geographical or cultural context where the antecedent text is believed to have originated; include name of codicologist in round brackets after each date range; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;).

Credit for Antecedent Reference/Counter-Tradition: Cite individual(s) or institution(s) who have previously established or mentioned an intertextual relationship between the two sources under analysis

Credit for MS Name: Attribution for the manuscript’s identification or catalog entry; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;)

MS Name: The manuscript’s official title or catalog number; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;)

Archive: The archive, library, or institution that holds the manuscript; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;).

MS Language: The language(s) in which the manuscript is written; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;).

Preserved Text: The specific portion of text that has survived; specify whether your given numbers relate to lines, sections or verses.

Reference to MS: Catalog reference, shelf mark, or URL linking to scan.

Reference to MS Transcription: Citation or link to a published or digital transcription of the manuscript.

Date Range for Production of Antecedent/Counter-Tradition: The estimated time period during which the antecedent text is thought to have been composed. Provide citations for further readings.

Date Range for Production of MS: The time period during which the manuscript is thought to have been created.

Translator: The name of the person responsible for translating the specific antecedent text we are working with; if this is an eclectic translation, add the word “eclectic” in brackets.

Reference to Translation: Citation or link to the translated version of the antecedent.

Parallel Passages:Indicate whether parallel or similar passages exist elsewhere in the Qur’an.

Parallel Passages (List): List specific parallel verses or passages; separate with semicolons (;).

Related Passages: Indicate whether there are thematically related verses elsewhere in the Qur’an.

Related Passages (List): List the related verses; separate with semicolons (;).

Frequent Formula: Indicate whether the textual segment includes a recurring phrase or formula (e.g., “In the name of God …”).

Frequent Formula Text: The actual phrase identified as recurring, if “Yes” is selected above.

Interpolation: Any later additions or insertions within the text; separate with semicolons (;).

Earliest Antecedent: Indicates that a particular antecedent is the earliest source of this particular trend or tradition.

Counter-tradition: Evidence of interpretation of Scripture or another source unlike or contradictory to the interpretation that developed into the Qur’an, including polemic traditions (i.e. a text or interpretation that deliberately contradicts or critiques another tradition); separate multiple entries with semicolons (;).

Further Readings: Short reference to any book or article that could be helpful in understanding the passage or topic under analysis; separate multiple entries with semicolons (;).
